Privacy & Terms

What Relay actually does with your data — written to match the code, not a generic template.

Last updated: June 21, 2026

1. What Relay is

Relay is a small, self-hosted real-time messaging web app. It runs securely on Google Firebase architecture. If you're using a Relay instance someone else set up, that person or organization controls the Firebase project your data lives in.

2. Information we store

Creating an account and using Relay stores the following in that Firebase project's Authentication and Firestore database:

  • Account info: your name, email address, and (if you sign in with Google) the profile photo Google provides.
  • Public profile: your display name, email, avatar, account creation time, and a "last active" timestamp, stored in a users collection so other people can find you to start a conversation.
  • Messages: text messages, image attachments, and voice messages you send. Images and voice messages are compressed in your browser before upload but are not encrypted — they are stored as plain Firestore data.
  • Message metadata: who sent a message, when, emoji reactions, and read receipts.
  • Friend requests and friends list: if you send or receive a friend request, the two email addresses involved and the request status are stored.
  • Blocked users: if you block someone, their email address is added to a private blocked list. They are not notified.
  • Local device data: your light/dark theme choice, and — only if you check "Remember me" — your email address, are stored in localStorage.

3. What we don't do

  • No analytics, ad tracking, or third-party tracking scripts of any kind are included in Relay's code.
  • No advertising — Relay doesn't show ads or sell data to advertisers.
  • No message content is end-to-end encrypted.

4. Third parties involved

Relay's code only ever talks to two outside services, governed by their own privacy policies:

  • Google Fonts — loads the typefaces used on every page.
  • Firebase / Google Cloud — provides account authentication and the Firestore database that stores profiles, messages, and friend data.

5. Your choices and controls

  • Edit your profile (display name, photo) any time from Settings.
  • Delete a message you sent at any time from the chat view.
  • Remove a friend any time from the Friends panel.
  • Block or unblock someone any time from a conversation's "⋯" menu.
  • Delete your account from Settings → Danger Zone. This removes your local account record, deletes your public profile, and deletes the authentication account.

6. Age limitations

Relay requires users to be at least 13 years of age. We do not knowingly collect or maintain information from individuals under this threshold. If an account is discovered to belong to a minor under 13, it will be terminated immediately.

7. Terms of use & Contact

  • Relay is provided on an "as is" and "as available" basis without operational warranties or guaranteed service uptime.
  • Users are entirely responsible for keeping their login credentials secure and for any data transmitted through their active sessions.
  • Data transmitted through Relay is stored in a cloud environment and is not end-to-end encrypted; avoid distributing high-sensitivity operational or personal information.
  • For safety notices, abuse complaints, or data removal requests, please submit an issue or contact the support desk via our official project distribution repository.
